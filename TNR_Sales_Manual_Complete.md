### 4.4 Pricing Strategy and Negotiation Guidelines

#### When to Discount

**Never discount more than 10%** unless:
- Client is signing 12+ month contract
- Client is bundling 3+ services
- Client is referring 2+ businesses immediately
- It's end of month/quarter and you need to hit quota

**Discount Framework:**
- **5% discount**: For 12-month commitment
- **10% discount**: For bundle of 3+ services OR referral of 2+ businesses
- **15% discount**: For both (12-month commitment + bundle + referrals)

**How to Present Discounts:**
*"I can offer you a 10% discount if you commit to a 12-month contract. That brings your Premium Package from $1,500/month to $1,350/month—saves you $1,800/year. Plus, you're locked into current pricing, so if we raise rates next year, you're protected. Sound fair?"*

#### Payment Terms

**Standard Terms:**
- Initial payment: First month + setup fee (if applicable) due upon signing
- Monthly payments: Auto-draft on 1st of each month
- Payment methods: ACH (preferred), credit card (3% fee)

**Payment Plans for Large One-Time Fees:**
- Web design: Can split into 3 payments (1/3 at signing, 1/3 at midpoint, 1/3 at launch)
- Example: $600 site = $200 at signing, $200 at week 2, $200 at launch

**What to Say:**
*"I know $600 upfront can be a lot. We can break it into payments: $200 today to start, $200 at the midpoint (week 2), $200 at launch. That way you're paying for progress, not promises. Does that work better for your cash flow?"*

#### Handling Price Objections

**Objection: "That's more expensive than [competitor]"**

Response: *"I hear you. Can I ask—what's included in their price? Because our $1,500/month Premium Package includes: unlimited website, full SEO suite, social media management, email marketing, AND paid ad management. Many agencies charge $1,500 just for SEO, then another $800 for social, $400 for email. That's $2,700 total. We bundle it all for $1,500. Plus, you get a dedicated manager—one person who knows your business inside and out. Would you like me to break down exactly what's included so you can compare apples to apples?"*

**Objection: "I can't afford $1,500/month"**

Response: *"I totally understand. Let's start smaller. What's your budget? If you can do $500/month, we can start with the Starter Package at $300/month plus basic SEO at $400/month. That's $700 total, but I can give you 10% off if you commit to 6 months—brings it to $630/month. Then, once you start seeing results and revenue increases, we can upgrade to Premium. **Objection: "I need to think about it"**

Response: *"Of course. What specifically do you want to think about? Is it the price, the services, or something else? Because if it's price, we can adjust. If it's services, we can customize. If it's timing, we can push the start date. I want to make sure this works for you—what's the main concern?"*

## Chapter 5: Sales Processes and Workflows

### 5.1 End-to-End Sales Workflow

#### Stage 1: Prospecting (Days 1-3)

**Goal:** Identify 20+ qualified leads per day

**Activities:**
- Research businesses on Google Maps (filter by industry, location)
- Scrape LinkedIn for decision-makers (CEO, Owner, Marketing Manager titles)
- Review Chamber of Commerce directory
- Check competitor websites for client lists
- Drive around local business districts (note businesses, take photos)

**Tools:**
- HubSpot CRM (log all leads)
- Google Maps
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (if available)
- Local business directories

**Qualification Checklist (Must Have 3+):**
- [ ] Located in Westmoreland County (or surrounding)
- [ ] Has website (even if outdated) OR actively needs one
- [ ] Industry matches ICP (retail, restaurant, contractor, healthcare, etc.)
- [ ] Appears to have budget indicators (professional office, active social media, recent reviews)
- [ ] Decision-maker contact info available (or can be found)

**Action:** Log in HubSpot with lead score (1-5, 5 = hot lead)

#### Stage 2: Initial Outreach (Days 1-5)

**Goal:** Get response, book demo, or disqualify

**Methods (Use Multiple):**

**1. Cold Call (Primary Method)**
- Script: See Chapter 7
- Target: 20 calls/day
- Best times: 9-11 AM, 2-4 PM (avoid lunch, early morning, late afternoon)

**2. In-Person Visit (Local Businesses)**
- Script: See Chapter 7
- Target: 2-10 visits/day (depending on location density)
- Best times: 10 AM-2 PM (avoid rush hours)
- Bring: Business cards, one-page service overview, tablet for live demos

**3. Email (Follow-Up to Call/Visit)**
- Template: See Appendix A.2
- Target: 20 emails/day
- Personalization: Mention specific pain point from research (e.g., "I noticed your site isn't ranking for '[service] near me'")

**4. LinkedIn Connection**
- Target: 20 connections/day
- Message: Personalized (reference their business, ask question)
- Follow-up: If they accept, send value-first message (case study, free audit offer)

**Response Handling:**
- **Hot Lead (Interested Now)**: Book demo immediately (same day or next day)
- **Warm Lead (Interested Later)**: Add to 7-touch sequence (see Chapter 10)
- **Cold Lead (Not Interested)**: Ask "What would need to change for you to be interested?" If no clear path, disqualify and move on.
- **No Response**: Add to 7-touch sequence

#### Stage 3: Qualification Call/Demo Booking (Days 2-7)

**Goal:** Confirm fit, identify pain points, book demo

**Discovery Questions:**
1. "How long have you been in business?" (Establishes stability)
2. "What's your biggest challenge with online visibility right now?" (Identifies pain)
3. "How are you currently getting customers?" (Current marketing methods)
4. "What would ideal online presence look like for you?" (Desired outcome)
5. "What's your timeline for improving this?" (Urgency)
6. "What's your budget range for marketing?" (Budget qualification)

**Qualification Criteria:**
- ✅ Budget: Can afford $300+/month minimum
- ✅ Authority: Decision-maker or can get them on demo
- ✅ Need: Has clear pain point (low traffic, outdated site, overpaying insurance)
- ✅ Timeline: Wants to move forward within 30 days

**If Qualified:**
- Book demo (next 2-7 days)
- Send calendar invite with Zoom link
- Send pre-demo questionnaire (optional, helps you prepare)

**If Not Qualified:**
- Be honest: "Based on what you've told me, I'm not sure we're the right fit right now. Can I check back with you in 6 months?"
- Still add to CRM (might qualify later)
- Ask for referral: "Even if we're not a fit, do you know any other business owners who might benefit from what we do?"

#### Stage 4: Demo Presentation (Days 7-14)

**Goal:** Show value, create urgency, close deal

**Demo Structure (15-20 minutes):**

**1. Opening (2 minutes)**
- Thank them for time
- Confirm their main pain point from qualification call
- Set agenda: "I'm going to show you exactly how we solve [their pain], then we'll talk about next steps. Sound good?"

**2. Proof (3 minutes)**
- Show case study (similar industry/business size)
- Highlight specific results (rankings, revenue, savings)
- Use screen-share to show actual results (Google rankings, traffic reports)

**3. Gap Analysis (5 minutes)**
- Live Google search: "Let's see where you rank right now for '[service] Greensburg'"
- Show their current ranking (probably page 2+ or not ranking)
- Compare to competitor rankings
- Calculate lost opportunity: "If you're getting 50 visitors/month but should be getting 200, that's 150 missed visitors. At a 5% conversion rate, that's 7.5 missed customers/month. At $150 average value, that's $1,125/month lost. That's $13,500/year walking out the door."

**4. Solution Presentation (5 minutes)**
- Present recommended package (based on their needs/budget)
- Show mockup or example (if web design)
- Explain timeline ("We'll have your site live in 3 weeks, ranking improvements in 60 days")
- Address ROI: "At $1,500/month, you're investing $18,000/year. But if we add just $13,500 in revenue (the opportunity we identified), you're net positive in year 1. Plus, that revenue continues year after year."

**5. Objection Handling (As Needed)**
- Use framework from Chapter 9

**6. Close (3-5 minutes)**
- Assumptive close: "So, we can get started next week. Which package makes the most sense for you—Starter or Premium?"
- If hesitation: "What's the one thing holding you back?"
- Address final concern, then close again

**Demo Tools:**
- Zoom (screen-share enabled)
- Google (for live searches)
- Canva mockups (for web design presentations)
- Case study PDFs (have 3-5 ready)
- Calculator (for ROI calculations)

**Post-Demo Actions:**
- Send follow-up email within 1 hour (thank you, recap, next steps)
- Send proposal (if not closed on demo)
- Schedule follow-up call (if needed)
- Log in HubSpot (notes, next steps, probability)

#### Stage 5: Proposal and Negotiation (Days 10-18)

**When to Send Proposal:**
- After demo (if not closed immediately)
- When client requests it
- As follow-up to objection ("Let me send you a detailed proposal")

**Proposal Components:**

1. **Cover Page**
   - Client name, date, proposal number
   - Your contact info

2. **Executive Summary**
   - Brief overview of their situation
   - Recommended solution
   - Expected outcomes

3. **Current Situation Analysis**
   - What you found (gap analysis from demo)
   - Quantified pain (lost revenue, missed opportunities)

4. **Recommended Solution**
   - Specific package/services
   - Timeline
   - Deliverables (what they get)

5. **Investment**
   - Total cost (monthly and annual)
   - Payment terms
   - ROI projection

6. **Why TNR**
   - USPs (local expertise, proven results, integrated services)
   - Case studies (2-3 relevant ones)

7. **Next Steps**
   - What happens after they sign
   - Timeline for kickoff
   - Contact info for questions

**Proposal Delivery:**
- Email PDF (professional format)
- Follow-up call within 24 hours: "Did you get a chance to review the proposal? Any questions?"

**Negotiation Guidelines:**
- If they ask for discount: "What would make this work for you? If it's budget, we can start with Starter Package. If it's timeline, we can adjust. If it's services, we can customize. What's the main concern?"
- Don't discount unless you get something (longer contract, referrals, immediate start)
- Always reinforce value before discounting

### 5.3 Common Process Roadblocks and Solutions

**Roadblock 1: Can't Get Past Gatekeeper**

**Problem:** Receptionist/assistant won't connect you to decision-maker

**Solutions:**
- Build rapport: "Hi, I'm [Name] with TNR. I help local businesses grow online. Is [Owner Name] available for a quick call?"
- Offer value: "I have a free website audit I'd like to share. Who should I talk to about that?"
- Be specific: "I'm calling about their Google ranking—they're not showing up when people search '[service] near me'. Who handles their marketing?"
- Leave voicemail with value: "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with TNR. I noticed [Business] isn't ranking on Google for '[keyword]'—I can show you exactly why and how to fix it. Call me back at [number]."
- Follow up with email: Reference the call, include case study, offer free audit

**Roadblock 2: Client Wants to "Think About It"**

**Problem:** Interested but won't commit

**Solutions:**
- Find the real objection: "I totally understand. What specifically do you want to think about? Is it the price, the services, the timeline?"
- Create urgency: "I have 2 spots left this month. After that, we're booked for 3 weeks. Want to lock in your spot?"
- Offer trial/test: "What if we started with a free audit? I'll show you exactly what's wrong, then you can decide if you want us to fix it."
- Set specific follow-up: "I'll call you back on [date] to see what you decided. Does [date/time] work?"
- Provide additional value: Send case study, ROI calculator, competitor analysis

**Roadblock 3: Price Too High**

**Problem:** Client likes solution but can't afford it

**Solutions:**
- Start smaller: "What's your budget? We can start with Starter Package at $300/month, then upgrade as you grow."
- Payment plan: "We can split the upfront cost into 3 payments."
- ROI focus: "I know $1,500/month seems like a lot. But if we add $13,500/year in revenue (which is conservative based on what we discussed), you're net positive. Plus, that revenue continues year after year."
- Discount for commitment: "If you commit to 12 months, I can give you 10% off—brings it to $1,350/month."
- Compare to cost of inaction: "Right now you're losing $13,500/year by not ranking. That's $1,125/month. Our service costs $1,500/month, but it fixes that $1,125/month loss PLUS adds new revenue. You're essentially investing $375/month to add $10k+/year in revenue."

**Roadblock 4: Client Already Has Agency**

**Problem:** "We're already working with someone"

**Solutions:**
- Don't badmouth competitor: "That's great! Are you happy with the results you're getting?"
- Ask about results: "What kind of results are you seeing? Rankings, traffic, leads?"
- Offer audit: "Can I run a free audit and show you where there might be opportunities they're missing? No obligation—just want to see if there's a gap."
- Position as complement: "We're not here to replace them. But many businesses work with multiple agencies—one for SEO, one for ads, one for social. We do it all, which might save you money and simplify things. Want to compare?"
- Timing: "When does your contract with them end? I can check back then."

**Roadblock 5: Low Demo Show Rate**

**Problem:** Prospects book demos but don't show up

**Solutions:**
- Send calendar reminder (24 hours before, 1 hour before)
- Confirm day before: "Hi [Name], looking forward to our call tomorrow at [time]. We're going to cover [agenda]. See you then!"
- Make it valuable: "I've prepared a custom analysis for your business. Excited to share it!"
- Reschedule easily: "If something comes up, just let me know—we can reschedule."
- Follow up on no-shows: "Hi [Name], I noticed we missed each other. Want to reschedule? I have [times] available."

### 6.2 Lead Qualification Framework

#### BANT Qualification (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)

**Budget:**
- Question: "What's your budget range for marketing/insurance?"
- Red flags: "We don't have a budget," "We can't afford that," "$50/month maximum"
- Green flags: "$500-2,000/month," "Depends on ROI," "What do you recommend?"

**Authority:**
- Question: "Are you the decision-maker, or do you need to run this by someone?"
- Red flags: "I need to ask my partner/spouse/boss," "I can't make this decision"
- Green flags: "I make the decisions," "I can sign today," "Let me check with [person] but I'll decide"

- Question: "What's your biggest challenge with [area]?"
- Red flags: "We're doing fine," "No challenges," "Everything is perfect"
- Green flags: "We're not ranking on Google," "Our site is outdated," "We're overpaying for insurance"

**Timeline:**
- Question: "When do you want to see results?"
- Red flags: "Maybe in a year," "No rush," "Just gathering info"
- Green flags: "Within 30 days," "As soon as possible," "This quarter"

**Scoring:**
- All 4 = Hot lead (prioritize, follow up daily)
- 3 of 4 = Warm lead (add to 7-touch sequence)
- 2 of 4 = Cold lead (nurture, check back quarterly)
- 1 of 4 = Disqualify (not a fit, ask for referral)

- Meets 3 of 6 criteria

- Meets 2 of 6 criteria

**1 = Poor Match:**

## Chapter 8: Creating Pain Points and Developing Needs

### 8.1 The Pain Funnel (3-Step Process)

#### Step 1: Identify Current State

**Questions to Ask:**
1. "How many visitors does your website get per month?"
2. "Where do most of your customers come from right now?"
3. "When people search '[service] near me' on Google, do you show up on page 1?"
4. "What's your website's bounce rate?" (if they know)
5. "How much are you spending on advertising right now?"
6. "What's your cost per lead?"
7. "When was the last time you shopped your insurance rates?"

**Listen For:**
- Low numbers (traffic, leads, rankings)
- Frustration ("We're not getting enough leads")
- Confusion ("I don't know why we're not ranking")
- Cost concerns ("We're spending too much on ads")

#### Step 2: Quantify the Pain

**The Math:**
- **Example 1: Low Traffic**
  - Current: 50 visitors/month
  - Should be: 200 visitors/month (based on search volume for their keywords)
  - Gap: 150 missed visitors/month
  - At 5% conversion: 7.5 missed customers/month
  - At $150 average value: $1,125/month lost = $13,500/year

- **Example 2: Low Rankings**
  - Current: Page 3 (rankings 21-30)
  - Should be: Page 1 (rankings 1-10)
  - 75% of people never click past page 1
  - If they're getting 50 visitors/month from page 3, they could get 200+ from page 1
  - 4x increase = $54,000/year opportunity (using example above)

- **Example 3: High Ad Spend, Low ROI**
  - Current: $1,200/month on ads, 10 leads/month = $120/lead
  - Should be: $800/month on ads, 25 leads/month = $32/lead
  - Savings: $400/month + 2.5x more leads
  - Annual value: $4,800 saved + $27,000 in new revenue (15 additional leads × $150 × 12 months)

- **Example 4: Insurance Overpayment**
  - Current: $2,100/month for auto/home/business (3 different agents)
  - Should be: $1,650/month (bundled with one agent)
  - Savings: $450/month = $5,400/year
  - Plus: One point of contact, coordinated coverage

**How to Present:**
*"So here's what I'm seeing: You're currently getting [X] visitors/month, but based on search volume for your keywords, you should be getting [Y] visitors/month. That's [gap] missed visitors. At a [conversion rate]% conversion rate, that's [missed customers] customers/month you're missing. At $[average value] per customer, that's $[monthly loss]/month, which is $[annual loss]/year walking out the door. Does that sound right?"*

#### Step 3: Prescribe the Solution

**Tie Pain to Package:**
*"Here's the good news: We can fix this. Our Premium Package does exactly what you need:*
- *We'll get you ranking on page 1 within 60-90 days (we've done it in 41 days)*
- *That'll get you from [current traffic] to [target traffic] visitors/month*
- *At your conversion rate, that's [new customers]/month*
- *At $[average value], that's $[new revenue]/month = $[annual revenue]/year*
- *The investment is $1,500/month, which is $18,000/year*
- *So you're investing $18k to add $[revenue], which means you're net positive in year 1. Plus, that revenue continues year after year. Does that make sense?"*

## Chapter 9: Objection Handling

### 9.1 The A.E.Q.C. Framework

**Acknowledge → Explore → Quantify → Close**

#### Step 1: Acknowledge

**Don't:** Argue, dismiss, or minimize their concern

**Do:** Validate their concern, show you understand

**Examples:**
- "I totally understand that concern."
- "That makes sense—a lot of people feel that way."
- "I hear you. Let me address that."
- "That's a valid point. Here's how we handle that..."

#### Step 2: Explore

**Don't:** Assume you know why they're objecting

**Do:** Ask clarifying questions to find the real objection

**Questions:**
- "What specifically concerns you about [objection]?"
- "Help me understand—is it the price, the services, or something else?"
- "What would need to change for you to feel comfortable with this?"
- "Have you had a bad experience with [similar service] before?"

**Common Hidden Objections:**
- "Too expensive" might mean: "I don't see the value" or "I need payment terms"
- "I need to think about it" might mean: "I need to talk to my partner" or "I'm not sure you can deliver"
- "We already have someone" might mean: "I'm happy with results" or "I don't want to hurt my current vendor's feelings"

#### Step 3: Quantify

**Don't:** Just explain—show the numbers

**Do:** Use data, case studies, ROI calculations

**Examples:**
- If price objection: Show ROI ("Yes, it's $1,500/month. But if we add $13,500/year in revenue, you're net positive.")
- If timeline objection: Show opportunity cost ("Every month you wait is $1,125 in lost revenue.")
- If results objection: Show case studies ("We did exactly this for [Client], and here are the results...")

#### Step 4: Close

**Don't:** End with explanation—always ask for commitment

**Do:** Present solution, address concern, then close again

**Examples:**
- "Does that address your concern?"
- "Now that we've covered that, are you ready to move forward?"
- "What other questions do you have, or are you ready to get started?"

### 9.2 Top Objections and Responses

#### Objection 1: "It's Too Expensive"

**Acknowledge:**
*"I totally understand. $1,500/month is a significant investment. Let me show you why it's worth it."*

**Explore:**
*"What's your budget range? Is it that you can't afford $1,500, or is it that you're not sure you'll get the value?"*

**Quantify:**
*"Here's the math: You're currently losing $13,500/year from not ranking on Google (we calculated that earlier). Our service costs $18,000/year, but it fixes that $13,500 loss PLUS adds new revenue. Most clients see $20k-30k in additional revenue within the first year. So you're investing $18k to add $20k-30k. That's a positive ROI in year 1, and that revenue continues year after year."*

**If Budget Constraint:**
*"What's your budget? If you can do $500/month, we can start with Starter Package at $300/month plus basic SEO at $400/month. That's $700 total, but I can give you 10% off if you commit to 6 months—brings it to $630/month. Then, once you start seeing results, we can upgrade. How does that sound?"*

**If Value Concern:**
*"What would need to happen for you to feel like you're getting $1,500/month in value? If we could get you ranking #1 and add $10k/year in revenue, would that be worth it?"*

**Close:**
*"Does that make sense? Are you ready to get started, or would you prefer to start with the smaller package?"*

#### Objection 2: "We Already Have a Website/SEO/Insurance"

**Acknowledge:**
*"That's great! Are you happy with the results you're getting?"*

**Explore:**
*"What kind of results are you seeing? Are you ranking on page 1? How much traffic are you getting? How many leads per month?"*

**Quantify:**
*"Let me check your current rankings... [Live search] I see you're on page 3, position 28. That means 99% of people searching for you won't find you. Plus, your site loads in [X] seconds—industry standard is 2 seconds. That's costing you [X]% of visitors who bounce immediately."*

**Alternative Approach:**
*"Even if you're happy with your current setup, we can complement it. Many businesses work with multiple agencies—one for SEO, one for ads, one for social. We do it all, which might save you money. Plus, we can do a free audit to see if there are opportunities your current agency is missing. No obligation—just want to see if there's a gap. Want me to run that audit?"*

**Close:**
*"Would you be open to a free audit to see if there are opportunities? Or, if you're completely happy with current results, that's fine—I'll check back in 6 months to see if anything's changed."*

#### Objection 3: "I Need to Think About It"

**Acknowledge:**
*"Of course. What specifically do you want to think about?"*

**Explore:**
*"Is it the price, the services, the timeline, or something else? Because if it's price, we can adjust. If it's services, we can customize. If it's timing, we can push the start date."*

**Quantify:**
*"I understand wanting to think it over. But here's the thing: Every day you're not ranking on Google is another day your competitors are getting customers who should be yours. If we calculated that you're losing $1,125/month, that's $37/day. Every day you wait is $37 walking out the door."*

**Create Urgency (If Appropriate):**
*"I have 2 spots left this month. After that, we're booked for 3 weeks. If you want to lock in your spot, I can hold it for 48 hours while you think about it. Does that work?"*

**Close:**
*"What specifically do you want to think about? Once we address that, are you ready to move forward? Or, if you really need time, I'll call you back on [date] to see what you decided. Does [date/time] work?"*

#### Objection 4: "We're Not Ready Right Now"

**Acknowledge:**
*"I understand. When would you be ready?"*

**Explore:**
*"What needs to happen for you to be ready? Is it budget, timing, or something else?"*

**Quantify:**
*"That makes sense. But here's something to consider: If you're not ready now, when will you be ready? And in the meantime, you're still losing [calculated loss]/month. If you wait 3 months, that's [3 × loss] you've lost that you'll never get back."*

**Alternative:**
*"What if we start smaller? Instead of the full Premium Package, we could start with just SEO at $400/month. That gets you ranking, and then when you're ready, we add the other services. That way you're not waiting—you're building momentum now."*

**Close:**
*"Does that work? Or would you prefer to wait? If you wait, I'll check back in [timeline] to see if you're ready then."*

#### Objection 5: "I Need to Talk to My Partner/Spouse/Boss"

**Acknowledge:**
*"Absolutely. Who else is involved in this decision?"*

**Explore:**
*"What are their main concerns? I'd love to address them before you talk to them so you're prepared."*

**Quantify:**
*"I get it. But while you're discussing it, you're still losing [calculated loss]/month. What if I could join the call with you and [person]? That way I can answer their questions directly, and we can make a decision right there."*

**Close:**
*"When can you talk to them? Can I schedule a call with all three of us? Or would you prefer to discuss it first and then get back to me? I'll follow up in [timeline] to see how it went."*

#### Objection 6: "Can You Guarantee Results?"

**Acknowledge:**
*"I understand wanting guarantees. Let me be honest about what we can and can't guarantee."*

**Explore:**
*"What specifically do you want guaranteed? Rankings? Revenue? Traffic?"*

**Quantify:**
*"Here's what I can guarantee:*
- *We'll improve your rankings measurably every month (if you're on page 3, we'll get you to page 2; if you're on page 2, we'll get you to page 1)*
- *We'll track everything and report monthly so you see progress*
- *If, after 90 days, you're not seeing improvements, we'll work with you to adjust the strategy at no extra cost*

*What I can't guarantee: Specific ranking positions (Google's algorithm changes constantly) or specific revenue numbers (too many variables outside our control).*

*But here's what I can show you: Our clients typically see 4-5x increase in traffic within 90 days, and most rank on page 1 within 60-90 days. We've done it in 41 days for some clients."*

**Close:**
*"Does that work for you? Are you comfortable with those guarantees?"*

#### Objection 7: "We Tried SEO/Marketing Before and It Didn't Work"

**Acknowledge:**
*"I'm sorry to hear that. What happened?"*

**Explore:**
*"What agency did you work with? What did they promise? What did they deliver? What went wrong?"*

**Quantify:**
*"That's frustrating. Here's what we do differently:*
- *We're local—we understand Greensburg and Westmoreland County*
- *We're transparent—monthly reports so you see exactly what we're doing*
- *We're proven—we've helped businesses rank #1 in 41 days*
- *We're integrated—one company, one point of contact, no finger-pointing*

*Plus, we can show you case studies of similar businesses we've helped. Want me to send those?"*

**Close:**
*"I know you've been burned before, but I'd love a chance to show you we're different. Can I do a free audit to show you what we'd do differently? No obligation—just want to prove our approach works."*

### 9.3 Objection Prevention

**Best Defense: Address Objections Before They Come Up**

**In Your Demo:**
- "I know you might be thinking 'This sounds expensive.' Let me show you the ROI..."
- "Some people worry about guarantees. Here's what we can and can't guarantee..."
- "If you're thinking 'We already have a website,' let me show you what's missing..."

**In Your Proposal:**
- Include FAQ section addressing common objections
- Show ROI calculations upfront
- Include guarantees/risk reversals

**In Your Follow-Up:**
- Anticipate objections and address them proactively
- "I know you might have concerns about [X]. Here's how we handle that..."

## Chapter 10: Closing Deals and Follow-Up Strategies

### 10.1 Closing Techniques

#### 1. Assumptive Close (Most Effective)

**How It Works:** Act as if they've already decided to buy.

**Example:**
*"Perfect! So we'll get you set up with the Premium Package. I'll send the contract via DocuSign—it takes about 5 minutes to sign. Once you sign, we'll schedule the kickoff call for next week. Your site will be live within 3 weeks, and you'll start seeing ranking improvements in 60 days. Sound good?"*

**When to Use:** When they've shown strong interest, asked about next steps, or you've addressed all objections.

#### 2. Alternative Close

**How It Works:** Give them two options, both of which involve buying.

**Example:**
*"So we have two options: The Starter Package at $300/month gets you the website and basic SEO—perfect if you want to start small and scale up. Or the Premium Package at $1,500/month gets you everything—website, full SEO, social media, email, ads, plus a dedicated manager. Which one makes more sense for where you are right now?"*

**When to Use:** When they're interested but unsure which package to choose.

#### 3. Urgency Close

**How It Works:** Create time-sensitive reason to buy now.

**Example:**
*"I have 3 spots left this month for new clients. After that, we're booked for 3 weeks. If we get started by Friday, you'll be live by [date] and ranking by [date]. If you wait, it'll be a 6-7 week delay. Want to lock in your spot?"*

**When to Use:** When you genuinely have limited availability or time-sensitive offer. Only use if true!

#### 4. Risk Reversal Close

**How It Works:** Remove their risk by offering guarantee or trial.

**Example:**
*"I know this is an investment, and I want you to feel confident. Here's what I'll do: If, after 90 days, you're not seeing the improvements we discussed—rankings moving up, traffic increasing, leads coming in—I'll work with you to adjust the strategy at no extra cost. We're not here to take your money and run—we're here to get you results. Does that help?"*

**When to Use:** When they're interested but hesitant about risk.

#### 5. Summary Close

**How It Works:** Summarize what they want and ask for confirmation.

**Example:**
*"Let me make sure I understand: You want to rank #1 for '[keyword]', you need a professional website that converts, and you want to start seeing results in 60 days. Our Premium Package does exactly that for $1,500/month. You're ready to move forward, right?"*

**When to Use:** At the end of demo or proposal review.

#### 6. Question Close

**How It Works:** Ask a question that implies they're buying.

**Example:**
*"What's your preferred start date—next week or the week after?"*
*"Should I send the contract to this email address?"*
*"Do you want to pay monthly or quarterly?"*

**When to Use:** When you're 90% sure they're ready to buy, but want final confirmation.

#### 7. Sharp Angle Close

**How It Works:** Agree to their request, but with condition of buying.

**Example:**
**Them:** *"Can you send me a detailed proposal?"*
**You:** *"Absolutely! I can send that today. And if the proposal looks good, are you ready to move forward and get started?"*

**When to Use:** When they ask for something (proposal, case study, reference) before deciding.

### 10.2 Reading Buying Signals

#### Verbal Signals (They're Ready to Buy)

- "How much does it cost?" (after you've shown value)
- "How long does it take?"
- "When can we get started?"
- "What's the next step?"
- "Do you work with businesses like ours?"
- "What do I need to do to get started?"
- "Can you customize this?"
- "What happens after I sign?"

#### Non-Verbal Signals (They're Ready to Buy)

- Leaning forward (engaged)
- Nodding (agreeing)
- Taking notes (serious)
- Asking about implementation (ready to move forward)
- Pulling out calendar (checking availability)

#### Red Flags (They're NOT Ready)

- Arms crossed (defensive)
- Checking phone repeatedly (disengaged)
- Looking away (not interested)
- "Let me think about it" (without specific concerns)
- Asking same question repeatedly (not understanding or not buying)
- Talking about competitors (shopping around, not serious about you)

**If You See Red Flags:**
- Pause and address: "I can tell you're not 100% sold. What's the main concern?"
- Don't force close—you'll get a cancellation or refund request

### 10.3 The 7-Touch Follow-Up Sequence

**Touch 1: Immediate (Day 1)** - Email with proposal, case study, ROI calculator
**Touch 2: Value Add (Day 2)** - Email/text with competitor analysis or urgency
**Touch 3: Social Proof (Day 4)** - Email with case study of similar business
**Touch 4: Competitor Focus (Day 7)** - Email/call about competitor threats
**Touch 5: Value Content (Day 10)** - Email with free guide or tip
**Touch 6: Final Push (Day 14)** - Call/email with free audit offer
**Touch 7: Bridge-Building (Day 21)** - Check-in email, re-engage

**After Touch 7:** Move to quarterly nurture or disqualify
